Column Definitions
MKT CAP
Market Capitalization — total market value of all shares outstanding (Price × Shares).
Mega >$200B · Large $10–200B · Mid $2–10B · Small <$2B
FAIR VALUE
Estimated intrinsic value per share based on analyst DCF models (via yFinance).
Green = price below fair value (potential upside) · Red = price above (potentially overvalued)
P/E
Price-to-Earnings — how much you pay for $1 of profit. (Price ÷ EPS)
Lower is cheaper, but compare within the same sector. Tech avg ~25x, Utilities ~15x.
P/B
Price-to-Book — price vs. net asset value. (Price ÷ Book Value per Share)
Under 1.0 = trading below asset value. High P/B is common in asset-light tech companies.
P/S
Price-to-Sales — price vs. revenue. (Price ÷ Revenue per Share)
Useful when P/E is unavailable (no earnings yet). Lower = potentially cheaper vs. revenue.
PEG
Price/Earnings-to-Growth — P/E adjusted for earnings growth rate. (P/E ÷ EPS Growth %)
Under 1.0 = potentially undervalued relative to growth. Best for growth stock screening.
GROSS MGN
Gross Margin — what % of revenue is left after cost of goods. ((Revenue − COGS) ÷ Revenue)
Higher is better. Software 70–90% · Retail 20–40% · Manufacturing 10–30%.
ⓘ About Data & Rate Limits
Data Source
All prices and fundamentals are pulled from Yahoo Finance (yFinance) — a free, public data service. No paid API keys are required, which makes this app free to run and safe to share. Data is for personal research and educational purposes only — not real-time trading data.
Refresh Schedule
Price updates
Every 60 seconds — pulled in batches of 50 symbols with a 3-second pause between batches to avoid triggering rate limits.
⚠ Yahoo Finance free data is delayed ~15 minutes during market hours — NOT real-time.
Fundamentals
Every 10 minutes — P/E, P/B, Fair Value, Gross Margin, etc. Each ticker fetches individually with a 1.2-second delay between requests.
184-ticker estimate
A full fundamentals pass across all tickers takes approximately 4 minutes to complete. This is intentional — slower fetching = fewer rate-limit blocks.
Rate Limits — What They Are & What to Expect
Yahoo Finance limits how many requests one IP address can make in a short window. Because this app runs on a shared hosting server (Render free tier), all users share the same outbound IP — so heavy usage can temporarily trigger a throttle.
⚠ You might see:
  • "Yahoo Finance rate limit — wait 30 seconds"
  • Prices showing as — briefly after import
  • Fundamentals not updated immediately on first load
  • Occasional blank P/E or Fair Value cells
✓ What the app does automatically:
  • Auto-retries once after 8 seconds on rate limit
  • Waits 45 seconds before retrying during background refresh
  • Spaces out all requests to minimize throttling
  • Data fills in on the next refresh cycle automatically
Tips for Best Experience
  • After importing a CSV, wait 2–5 minutes for all fundamentals to populate — the background fetcher works through them gradually.
  • If you see a rate-limit error when adding a ticker, wait 30 seconds and try again — the server auto-retries once already.
  • Prices update every 60 seconds during market hours. After hours prices may lag by 15–60 minutes depending on Yahoo's feed.
  • Fair Value (analyst target) and some fundamentals are not available for every ticker — ETFs and micro-caps often show —.
  • This app is not connected to any brokerage. Data is for research only — always verify with your broker before trading.
